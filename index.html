Conference Goals & Success Criteria
▾
1.1 Why We Attend Conferences
  • Pipeline generation or revenue influence
  • Brand awareness
  • Customer retention or expansion
  • Partner recruitment or enablement
  • Market intelligence (competitors, trends, feedback)
1.2 Defining Success

Before committing to an event, success metrics are defined as:

Lead Metrics
  • Booth traffic — leads scanned
  • Demos booked on-site
  • Time to meet (target: <14 days)
Pipeline Metrics
  • Pipeline created
  • Deals influenced (existing pipeline touched)
  • Post-event opportunities created
Content Metrics
  • Content captured on-site
  • Brand exposure generated
2
Conference Team & Attendance Criteria
▾
2.1 Who Attends

Attendance is determined by event objectives, expected business impact, and role alignment. AEs are the primary sales presence; BDRs provide support where appropriate.

Partner / Channel Events
Leadership, partner-facing Sales roles, and Marketing attend to support partner growth and strategic relationships.
Brand / Thought Leadership
Marketing and Leadership attend to support visibility and positioning, with selective Sales participation when appropriate.
2.2 Sales Participation Guidelines

Attendance for BDRs and AEs considers: alignment with event goals and ICP, presence of target accounts or pre-booked meetings, timing within the quarter and active deal cycles, and expected revenue impact vs. time out of market.

Account Executives
  • Primary sales presence at conferences
  • Focus on scheduling meetings, strategic conversations, and pipeline advancement
Business Development Reps
  • Supplementary support for lead capture and qualification
  • Assist with meeting scheduling and follow-up coordination
  • Support AEs in maximizing coverage
2.3 Fairness, Rotation & Approval
Sales participation requires alignment with the individual's manager. Final approval rests with Marketing and Leadership. Attendance opportunities are rotated when feasible — willingness to travel is considered but does not guarantee attendance.

Conference Setup & On-Site Expectations
▾
3.1 Booth Setup
Brand Display
  • Branded banner(s) & backdrop
  • Tablecloth & table tents
Engagement Tools
  • Mac desktop for demos
  • Tablets for meeting booking & lead capture
  • Collateral (one-pagers, pamphlets)
Extras
  • Swag

All team members are expected to assist with booth setup and breakdown.

3.2 Employee Expectations On-Site

Be present, engaged, and professional — actively initiating conversations, representing the brand, and capturing notes for follow-up.

Dress Code
  • Branded ¼ zip or polo
  • Dark jeans (no rips/stains/tears), black pants, or beige pants
  • Clean sneakers — white or black
Booth Conduct
  • No eating or chewing gum at the booth — water only
  • Other beverages away from the booth area
  • Alcohol only at designated networking events, always moderate
  • Booth must remain staffed at all times — coordinate before stepping away

Conference Assets & Materials
▾
4.1 Core Assets
  • Branded banner(s) & backdrop
  • Tablecloth & table tents
  • Collateral (one-pagers, pamphlets)
  • Mac desktop computer for demos
  • Tablets for booking meetings and capturing attendee information
  • Swag
The Events & Community Manager owns the inventory list and identifies gaps well before each event.
4.2 Non-Core & Event-Specific Assets
Vertical-Specific Materials
Printed or digital materials tailored to specific industries (Accounting, Pool Services, etc.) when the event audience requires targeted messaging.
Conference-Specific Offers
Special promos or incentives created exclusively for event attendees when aligned with Sales and Marketing strategy.
Speaking & Sponsored Sessions
Presentation decks, one-pagers, or signage for speaking engagements or sponsored sessions.
Campaign-Driven Materials
Assets tied to a specific campaign, product launch, or initiative being highlighted at the event.
4.3 Asset Planning & Approval

Non-core assets should be planned and approved 4–8 weeks in advance. The Events & Community Manager works with Marketing to determine what's needed for each event.

Pre-Conference Playbook
▾
1
Early Strategy & Amplification 8–12 weeks out
  • Identify vendors, partners, or collaborators for cross-marketing
  • Evaluate digital cross-promotion opportunities before, during, and after the event
  • Assess co-hosted events, meetups, or joint collateral with other vendors
  • Align internally on messaging, ownership, and execution for approved partner activities
2
Multimedia & Content Planning 8–12 weeks out
  • Determine whether videography and/or photography is needed
  • Define content objectives (brand, product, partner, or prospect)
  • Confirm availability and scope for multimedia support
  • Include Multimedia Content Creator in travel planning if required
3
Event Planning & Logistics 4–8 weeks out
  • Confirm event goals and success metrics
  • Finalize internal attendees
  • Secure sponsorship details and confirm all deliverables
  • Book travel and hotel accommodations
  • Confirm booth logistics, shipping requirements, and key deadlines
4
Execution Readiness & Coordination 2–4 weeks out
  • Align Sales and Marketing on goals, target accounts, and key messaging
  • Schedule meetings with prospects, customers, and partners
  • Confirm event-specific scheduling page is live and functioning
  • Determine if a shoulder event is appropriate — go/no-go at least 1 month prior
  • Finalize demos, talking points, and booth messaging
  • Review lead capture process and post-event follow-up expectations
5
Final Prep & On-Site Readiness 1 week out
  • Conduct internal event briefing covering goals, schedule, and expectations
  • Confirm on-site roles, booth coverage, and meeting ownership
  • Reconfirm travel, hotel, and shipping details
  • Ensure lead capture tools are tested and ready
6
Conference Communication Strategy
▾
6.1 Internal Communication
Before
  • Share conference details via Slack and the conference tracker (goals, attendees, key meetings)
  • Align teams on why the event matters and what success looks like
During
  • Share brief updates (demos booked, meetings scheduled)
  • Provide visibility into on-site activity and momentum
After
  • Document what worked, what didn't, and recommendations for next time
  • Distribute post-event recap (outcomes, results, next steps)
  • Share key takeaways, pipeline indicators, and feedback
6.2 External Communication
Before
  • Notify partners and prospects via email/outreach
  • Proactively identify who is attending the event
  • Schedule meetings and shoulder events with high-priority accounts
During
  • Engage attendees through pre-scheduled meetings and on-site conversations
  • Share light social or event presence where appropriate
After
  • Follow up with all meetings, leads, and relevant attendees
  • Tailor follow-up messaging based on event conversations
  • Reinforce next steps and engagement opportunities
6.3 Ownership & Alignment
Marketing
Owns overall conference communication strategy and coordination.
Sales
Owns direct outreach and meeting follow-up with assigned accounts.
Leadership
Participates in strategic or high-value communications as needed.
7
Post-Conference Playbook
▾
⚡
Immediate Actions 0–3 days post-event
  • Demos booked at the conference are automatically routed to the appropriate AE
  • All leads collected are routed to Sales by the Events & Community Manager
  • Marketing partners with Sales leadership to review, qualify, and assign leads to BDRs
  • Capture notes and context from on-site conversations for each lead
  • Send thank-you follow-ups to all partners, customers, and prospects met at the event
  • Complete a conference summary (overview, highlights, ROI)
📅
Short-Term Follow-Up 1–2 weeks post-event
  • Sales conducts timely follow-up on all assigned leads and meetings
  • Marketing launches coordinated follow-up campaigns aligned to event messaging
  • Meetings, demos, and next steps are scheduled as appropriate
📊
Long-Term Evaluation Ongoing
  • Track pipeline influence, opportunity progression, and closed-won revenue tied to the event
  • Gather feedback from Sales, Marketing, and attendees on event effectiveness

Your field reference for representing Alternative Payments at conferences — covering dress code, on-site conduct, networking, booth etiquette, competitor awareness, and what success looks like.

Representing the Company
▾

You are a brand ambassador at every event. How you dress, speak, and engage directly reflects on the company.

  • Be professional, approachable, and respectful at all times
  • Assume every interaction could influence a partnership, sale, or referral
  • When in doubt, default to being helpful and curious rather than salesy
👔
Dress Code
▾

All booth staff will wear the same branded gear — you'll receive clear guidance on what to wear each day.

✓ Wear
  • Branded ¼ zip or polo (no outdated logos)
  • Dark jeans (no rips/stains/tears), black or beige pants
  • Clean sneakers — white or black, suitable for long standing
  • Layers (exhibit halls are unpredictable)
✗ Avoid
  • Gym wear, cargo pants, overly distressed clothing
  • Flip flops, sandals, or high heels
  • Anything overly revealing or distracting
📍
On-Site Expectations
▾
Before
  • Review the event agenda and attendee list
  • Understand our goals (brand awareness, demos, partnerships)
  • Know our product, positioning, and current priorities
During
  • Arrive early — phones away during conversations
  • Take brief notes after meaningful conversations
  • Be mindful of alcohol consumption at social events
After
  • Share notable takeaways, leads, or feedback with the team
  • Flag any follow-ups that should happen quickly
💬
Networking & Conversation Starters
▾
Build Rapport First — Don't Pitch Immediately

Easy openers:

"What brought you to the conference?"
"Have you attended this event before?"
"What sessions are you most excited about?"
"What's been the most useful thing you've learned so far?"

Naturally introducing what we do:

"I'm with Alternative Payments — we work with MSPs on solving revenue workflow challenges. What about you?"
"A lot of folks here are talking about [relevant theme]. Is that something you're seeing too?"
Ask open-ended questions · Listen more than you talk · Avoid hard selling unless someone explicitly asks
🏪
Booth & Sponsored Event Etiquette
▾
  • Greet attendees warmly — eye contact and a smile go a long way
  • During exhibit hall hours, the booth must always be staffed — coordinate before stepping away
  • Avoid sitting, eating, or drinking anything other than water at the booth
  • Stay off your phone at the booth
  • Keep conversations concise when traffic is high
  • Know when to pull in another teammate for deeper questions
🔧
Booth Setup & Breakdown
▾

Setup and breakdown are team responsibilities regardless of role or title.

Setup
  • Arrive on time (or early)
  • Assist with unloading, assembling, organizing, and tidying
  • Ensure the booth is clean and ready before attendees arrive
  • If a task isn't obvious, ask how you can help
Breakdown
  • Plan to stay through the end of the event
  • Assist with packing, dismantling, and loading materials
  • Do not disappear early or assume others will handle it
If you have a conflict that impacts setup or breakdown, communicate it in advance with the Events & Community Manager.
❓
Handling Common Questions
▾

If you don't know the answer:

  • Be honest: "That's a great question — I want to make sure I get you the right answer."
  • Offer to connect them with the right person
  • Capture their info for follow-up
Never guess or overpromise.
🔍
Competitor Awareness
▾

You don't need to lead with competitive talk, but be prepared if prospects ask. Stay factual and respectful — focus on fit, tradeoffs, and customer experience.

ConnectBooster
Kaseya-owned, bundled approach
Multiple fees and systems can create client confusion
Fee pass-through often results in multiple invoices
WisePay
Bundled within ConnectWise
Historically lower adoption due to onboarding friction
Older customer portal experience
FlexPoint
Newer player in the market
Smaller footprint and support org vs. more established tools
QuickBooks Payments
Horizontal solution — not MSP-specific
Limited PSA-first workflows
No dedicated customer payment portal
"Different tools work for different MSPs. We tend to work best with teams who care about a clean payment experience and want to reduce friction for both their staff and their clients."
🤝
Professional Conduct
▾
Always
  • Be respectful to teammates, attendees, vendors, and event staff
  • Keep conversations inclusive and appropriate
  • Follow all event rules and venue policies
Never
  • Speak negatively about competitors, customers, or partners
  • Share confidential or internal-only information
  • Engage in arguments or heated discussions
🏆
Defining Success
▾

A successful conference isn't just about leads.

Relationships
Strong conversations and relationships built with prospects, partners, and peers.
Market Intelligence
Clear learnings about the market, customer pain points, or industry trends.
Brand Perception
Positive brand perception and memorable impressions left with attendees.
Actionable Follow-Ups
Follow-ups that convert conversations into pipeline after the event.
If you're unsure about expectations, attire, or goals for a specific event — reach out to the Events & Community Manager before the conference.
